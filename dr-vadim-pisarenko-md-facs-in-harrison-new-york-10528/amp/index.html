Good candidate for mommy makeover or should I lose weight first?
Mommy makeover candidate
Aug 11, 2018
Thank you for the question. Based on the pictures you provided I would recommend you lose some weight prior to undergoing surgery. Your final result would be significantly better at a weight of 175-185 lbs, for your height. Losing the weight would decrease the amount of your intra-abdiminal fat (fat around your intestines), which cannot be addressed with surgery. This would make your belly significantly flatter with a tummy tuck. Good luck!
When can I start working out after my BBL?
Exercise after BBL
Aug 11, 2018
In my practice I strongly encourage avoiding cardio 3-4 weeks post-op and weight lifting 6-7 weeks. Like with any prolonged absence from the gym, you should start slow and gradually increase intensity. Stiffness and muscle aches are normal in the beginning. Good luck on your journey...
I got Hyaluronidase to dissolve my lip fillers. My lips are huge and swollen huge. Is this normal?
Lip swelling after Hyaluronidase injection
Aug 11, 2018
Thank you for your question. Hyaluronidase frequently causes more swelling than the HA itself due to an inflammatory response. Swelling should resolve in 7-10 days. You may need re-injection of Hyaluronidase for symmetry and more even dissolution of HA. Good luck.
Do I have enough fat to lipo & inject into butt?
Enough fat for BBL.
Aug 11, 2018
Based on pictures alone it's very difficult to assess the amount of fat you have to use for transfer. An in person examination with a board certified plastic surgeon can answer that question more accurately, and provide some recommendations and devise a plan. Selective liposculpting of the lower back and flanks alone for example, can result in appearance of increased projection.
I'm scared to take my binder off, when am I safe to do so without ruining my results?
Removing binder after tummy tuck
Aug 11, 2018
I frequently recommend wearing the binder 6-8 weeks post-op. It will especially help during sleep and work. Removing it 6 weeks post-op to take a "peek" will not ruin your results. But as always, please discuss with your surgeon.
