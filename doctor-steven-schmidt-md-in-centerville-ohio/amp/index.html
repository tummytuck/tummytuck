What is the longevity of cohesive gel implants?
Cohesive gel implants
May 25, 2017
Only saline implants can be placed easily through an axillary approach. Nobody knows if cohesive gel implants will ever need to be replaced or not since they have not been around that long. The don’t leak but can fracture, usually when being put in. It will be 20 or 30 years before we know for sure how long they last. Almost all cohesive gel implants are placed through an incision below the breast fold approach. Implants are very easy to switch out through…

Tuberous breasts, implants, breast lift
May 25, 2017
I don’t think you have tuberous breasts. You only have ptosis, or drooping of the breasts. This would be treated by a breast lift. You could have implants placed if you want to be larger than you are now. If you are happy with your breast volume, no implants would be needed. Breast implants and breast lift can be performed at the same time in some patients depending on the size of implants and the degree of breast lift required.

Are my implants too high, too swollen & too hard?
Breast augmentation
May 20, 2017
It is very common for the pectoralis muscle to push the implants upwards. This occurs more often than not. The bandeau can help a great deal to speed the process of the implants dropping into the appropriate position. Some want to wear a bra in addition to the bandeau but this decreases its effectiveness. Try to be patient. They will get better.
Silicone implant safety? What about autoimmune disorders?
Safety of silicone implants
May 19, 2017
First, there is no metal in breast implants. There is a stainless steel backing in a breast tissue expander, but not an implant. The issue of auto immune diseases and breast implants was addressed by TV media during the 1990’s. Many women reported developing auto immune diseases after having breast implants placed. The problem with individual stories is they lack context. Who gets breast implants? Women. Who gets autoimmune disorders? Primarily women…
