Recovery time tummy tuck procedure

You will hear similar responses from all of us, but realistically, assuming uncomplicated healing, it will take about three weeks to feel about 75% recovered, and another three weeks to get to about 95%, including the lifting of most or all post-operative restrictions and the ability to resume exercise, jogging, etc.

Because of the risk of DVT (deep vein thrombosis) which exists with any lengthy surgery, we recommend avoiding any long flights or road trips about a week before your surgery, which could increase your risk, and staying very well hydrated right up to your surgery.

We also recommend attention to your bowel regimen to avoid postoperative constipation, which is common for many reasons including the use of narcotic analgesics and relative immobility for a few days after surgery.

(Ram Kalus, MD, Mount Pleasant Plastic Surgeon)

Return to flight attendant duties after a tummy tuck

The issue for you will be fatigue as well as ability to lift 40 pounds. If you have long distance flights that include stop-overs, especially in foreign countries where you have to deal with customs and security, or cross-country flights with 3 hour time changes, plan on 6 weeks – you are likely to be too tired to do such flights before then. You can always go back to work earlier, if you recover faster. If you have short flights – especially short hop round-trip flights, 4 weeks should be fine.

Recovery time tummy tuck scar

Be sure to wear knee high compression hose and a compression garment or girdle at work – being on your feet all day will lead to lower body swelling for 3 months after a tummy tuck. (Elizabeth Morgan, MD, PhD, Atlanta Plastic Surgeon)
