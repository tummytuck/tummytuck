The tummy tuck can really be 3 or 4 different operations depending on your examination. The goal is to tighten the muscles and remove unwanted skin and fat. Some women have widely spaced muscles (rectus diastasis, contributes to the “bulge” or “pooch” in the abdomen) while others have an unsightly scar and extra skin.

Some women have both of these problems. A mini tummy tuck, full tummy tuck without muscle repair, or full with muscle repair may be indicated. A vertical tummy tuck could be indicated in patients with previous vertical abdominal scars or those who have lost considerable weight from gastric bypass surgery.

So the cost really depends on your goals and the opinion of the plastic surgeon. Prices for these procedures may range from $6-12K in my practice.

Tummy tuck costs vary depending on the magnitude of the tuck and whether or not liposuction will be performed. Be cautious shopping for the lowest-cost provider.

You’re not buying a product; you’re buying a service which can vary greatly depending on the expertise of the surgeon. Find someone who you think will do a great job. It’s hardly a bargain if you get a cheap price and get a botched job and then need a revision. You could easily double the cost of the procedure if you need more work done to correct the mistakes of the first surgeon.

A tummy tuck can really vary from surgeon to surgeon and patient to patient. Some patients just have a small amount of excess skin/stretch marks below the belly button and may only require a mini-tummy tuck while others may have lost > 100 lbs and require an extensive procedure to remove all of the excess skin.

A typical tummy tuck typically involves tightening the abdomen by removing the excess skin, detaching/re-attaching the belly button, tightening the abdominal muscles (plication of diastasis) and often some liposuction of flanks/hips. In the Los Angeles area a full tummy tuck by a Board Certified Plastic Surgeon working at an Accredited Surgery Center including facility fee, surgeons fee, and anesthesia will likely run $8,000-20,000 (less for a mini).
