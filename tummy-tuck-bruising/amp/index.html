Tummy tuck bruising

Bruising is very common the first few weeks after a liposuction and tummy tuck procedure.

About 90% of the bruising will resolve by 3 weeks post procedure. The remaining swelling will continue to gradually improve over the next 3 months.

I find compression binders along with the use of arnica to be very helpful with bruising. (Paul S. Gill, MD, Houston Plastic Surgeon)

Healing bruises after a tummy tuck take time

The tummy tuck bruising

Tummy tuck combined with liposuction is a surgical procedure that takes some time to recover from. Swelling and bruising are both part of a normal recovery process. There is a broad range of recovery time, mostly dependent on the patient’s medical history, health status, use of medications, etc. Routinely, the initial healing takes 2-3 weeks and the final scar maturation takes up to six months. Some patients use Arnica gel to speed up resolution of post operative bruising, even though there is no scientific support to this claim. (Z. Paul Lorenc, MD, Manhattan Plastic Surgeon)

Bruising Following Abdominoplasty & Liposuction May Last Up to 6 Weeks

Abdominoplasty bruising

A tummy tuck bruising

It’s not uncommon for patients who undergo abdominoplasty in combination with liposuction to experience bruising in the post-operative period. In the vast majority of cases, this is usually related to the liposuction component of the procedure. This bruising is typically a self limited phenomenon that lasts about two weeks.

In rare cases, it might last for up to six weeks following surgery. Bruising varies from patient to patient. Many patients have no bruising at all, while others occasionally experience severe prolonged bruising.

It’s important to remember that bruising resolves with time. Therefore, be patient and make sure you discuss all your concerns with your plastic surgeon. (Richard J. Bruneteau, MD, Omaha Plastic Surgeon)

Be patient, bruising may take 4 weeks to fade

The bruising is likely mainly due to the lipo and not the tummy tuck. It may take 3 or even four weeks for all of the bruising to fade. Hang in there. You really can’t speed up healing, but you can impede it. You have had a major operation and your body needs time to mend.

Be sure to keep hydrated, keep up your calorie and proteins, move. Your results look great so far. By the way, don’t be tempted to do too much, you will need about 6 weeks for the strength to build in the repaired areas. (Scott E. Kasden, MD, Dallas Plastic Surgeon)
