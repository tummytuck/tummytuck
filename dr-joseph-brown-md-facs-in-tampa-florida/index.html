Why would you need a mesh in a breast augmentation procedure?
"mesh" with breast augmentation
Oct 23, 2017
It is very uncommon to use any type of "mesh" or "extra tissue" during a primary breast augmentation, meaning the first time a patient has had implants placed. It is typically used to provide extra support in situations where a revision surgery is required for visible rippling due to thin tissue, implant malposition (implant falling out into the armpit or down low on to the upper abdomen), or symmastia ("uniboob").
Good luck!

timing for scar revision
Oct 12, 2017
The key to a good scar is meticulous closure and patience. Your new scar has begun the entire healing process all over again. It's normal for scars to become pink and puffy as your body is mending itself. I wouldn't consider another revision for at least six months, but preferably a year, if it's necessary. Make sure you address your concerns with your surgeon, just like you did before, and listen to their advice.
