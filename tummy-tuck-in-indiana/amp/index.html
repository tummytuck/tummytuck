Preparing for tummy tuck in Indiana

The plastic surgeon will give you individual instruction in preparation for the tummy tuck, including guidelines on eating, smoking, alcohol intake, and taking or avoiding certain vitamins and medications.

Procedure for tummy tuck

Full tummy tuck at Indiana usually takes from two to three hours, depending on the amount of surgery. Partial tummy tuck may take from one to two hours.

After tummy tuck surgery Indiana

Most often during abdominoplasty surgery, the surgeon makes a long incision from one hip bone to the other just above the pubic area. A second incision is made to separate the umbilicus from surrounding tissues. With partial tummy tuck at Indiana incision is much shorter. Next, the surgeon separates the skin from the abdominal wall to gain access to vertical muscles of the abdomen.

Then, these muscles are tightened and fixed in the new position. This provides greater flexibility of the abdominal wall and narrows the waist. The excess skin is removed, forming a new hole for the navel. Once the incisions are sewn, applied dressings.

With partial tummy plastic at Indiana skin is separated only between the incision line and the navel. This flap extends downward, and the excess skin is removed.

Surgeons of our clinic owned exclusive technology to reduce the length of the scar in the abdomen and is often used laser liposuction during surgery.

Tummy tuck after surgery

During the first few days after tummy tuck at Indiana in place of surgery will be swelling. Pain and discomfort are minimized with medication. Depending on the complexity of of surgery, you allowed to go in a few hours or be accommodated in a comfortable hospital for one or two days.

Your doctor will guide you throughout the process of recovery from surgery, will provide all the necessary instructions (like taking a shower, change bandages, etc..), Will help you choose a set of physical exercises for a speedy recovery.
