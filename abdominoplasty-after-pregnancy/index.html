Pregnancy fat – Liposuction or Abdominoplasty

Pregnancy fat is tough. As a plastic surgeon and mom of three young kids, I understand both sides of the post baby body – as a surgeon and a woman.

Abdominoplasty surgery after pregnancy

  1. Give yourself time to get your body back in shape. You need to get a good diet and exercise routine. And with young children, especially close together, it can be impossible to devote any time to you. Ignore magazines with photos of movie stars back to their “pre baby shape in two months!” The movie stars have trainers, special meals, nannies, airbrushing, and sometimes surgeries (which I would definitely NOT recommend right after a baby, especially if breast feeding.)
  2. Don’t decide on which procedure is right due to cost.
  3. Meet with two plastic surgeons, board certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery. A dermatologist who does lipo won’t recommend an abdominoplasty after pregnancy because he can’t do a tummy tuck. You need someone who can objectively evaluate what is best for you. Liposuction, even laser lipo, Smart Lipo, Slim Lipo, Vaser, UAL will NOT tighten the skin much.

The marketing hype is a flashy gimmick to draw you in. If your skin is loose now, it will be looser after removing fat. If you need an abdominoplasty after pregnancy, I strongly recommend you do that. I have seen many women who had liposuction and then come to me for a tummy tuck. They have horrible irregular scarred skin, which for many, I can’t fix. They needed the tummy tuck in the first place. (Lauren Greenberg, MD, Palo Alto Plastic Surgeon)
