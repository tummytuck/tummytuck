Cost of tummy tuck

Cost varies by your surgeon’s experience, the size of your case and your surgeon’s practice philosophy. One surgeon’s tummy tuck is not equivalent to another’s. It also varies by your location. Some areas of the country are also more expensive than others.

The range here is substantial, but a good mid-point is around $10,000. Get several estimates and then compare for your situation and location.

The cost of a tummy tuck is somewhat dependent upon where you live, somewhat dependent upon the surgeon, somewhat dependent upon where it is performed and somewhat dependent upon the details of your surgery. Most centers will have a standard cost that can go up or down based on your specific characteristics. At our center it is between $5000 and $12000 with most being in the $7000-$8000 range.

Additional liposuction or more complex procedures, such as a Fleur-de-lis or circumferential lower body lift can move the cost to a higher number. As with most things in plastic surgery, a high or low cost is not indicative of quality and should absolutely not be the reason you decide upon a certain surgeon.

If cost is a really important issue, speak with a number of board certified surgeons, seek out the expertise of someone who does many different kinds of tummy tucks and who you trust. A $4000 dollar surgery from someone you don’t love in an uncomfortable or unclean environment is rarely a better option than paying a few thousand dollars more for a top flight experience.

The price of a tummy tuck can vary depending on the specifics of each patient’s anatomy and the different techniques that are needed to transform you into your ideal form. Some patients only require a mini tummy tuck ($4600.00) if the majority of their problem is below the belly button and they are close to their ideal weight. This can be combined with liposuction if there is excess fatty tissue extending in the hip and thigh areas (mini tummy tuck with liposuction $5150.00).

If your problem is more widespread on the abdomen and involves excess skin and tissue both above and below the belly button or if there is a separation of the muscles (as is common after pregnancy or weight gain) then a full tummy tuck may be required ($5700.00).

Also, if fatty tissue involves the hips and thighs, a full tummy tuck may be combined with liposuction to give maximal improvement of that region (fully tummy tuck with liposuction of hips and thighs $6900.00).
