Does Scarring Ever Fade Completely After a Breast Lift?
Does scarring ever fade completely after a breast lift?
Jan 11, 2018
Scar tissue is the glue that heals our bodies. Scars will fade significantly over the years however, you will always have a hint of a scar. We take every effort to minimize the size and area of the incision. We recommend using a scar reducing ointment post surgery to obtain maximum fading.
Breast Lift Without Implants?
Breast Lift without Implants?
Jan 11, 2018
Yes you can have a breast lift without implants. Many women have enough breast tissue to lift and still have a full looking breast without the need for an implant. Your doctor can determine this before your surgery.
Acrylic nails and nail polish during surgery?
Acrylic nails and nail polish during surgery?
Jan 11, 2018
Most surgery centers prefer you do not. Your oxygen levels are read through your nail beds throughout the surgery and recovery. Polish or acrylic nails enables your anesthesiologist to monitor your levels. Some centers may use a ear piece monitor as an alternative. Your surgeon will inform you as to what their center requirements are.
Phlegm After Surgery?
Phlegm after surgery?
Jan 11, 2018
It is normal to experience an increase in secretion post-surgery. The medication typically administered during surgery will dry up your secretion for airway safety. Post-surgery you will experience an increase as your body re-hydrates.

Is a Benelli lift the same thing as a SPAIR lift? (Form of mastopexy). Will it flatten the nipp
Benelli vs SPAIR breast lift
Jun 16, 2017
Good question.
A Benelli lift is a procedure that uses a per-areolar incision. It frequently has a flattening affect in the nipple areolar area. The SPAIR technique is a type of reduction. It utilizes a short scar (circumvertical or "lollipop") incision. The short scar procedures tend to give more lift and better projection.
I hope this helps.

How Long After Breast-feeding Can I Get Breast Implants?
Breast Augmentation after Breast Feeding
Jul 11, 2012
Typically I advise my patients to wait at least 3 to 6 months after they stop breastfeeding, this will allow for the breast tissue to settle down from lactating and the breast to reach the final post partum size. Some women can still express a small amount of milk up to 9 months after breast feeding. Schedule a consultation with a board certified plastic surgeon to evaluate your options further.
