Do I need to worry? Does this need wound care other than cleansing and keeping dry?
Wound Care
Jun 5, 2019
This is fairly common for large breast reductions. I would recommend cleaning the wound with soap and water. Once it is clean and dry, apply a thin layer of OTC antibiotic ointment and a dry gauze. Doing this daily should help the wound heal. If the wound does not continue to get better or this area becomes red/hot to touch and you start having fevers you should contact your plastic surgeon.

Sharp Pains
Jun 5, 2019
Frequent sharp pains are usually associated with nerve pain. The nerves in your breast and nipple were cut during surgery. While these nerves are growing back and recovering, you can experience frequent sharp pains.
Can Latisse make your eyelashes fall out? I've been using it for probably 6 months now.
Eyelash Loss
Jun 5, 2019
I have heard patients report this. Once you reach the length and thickness of your lashes you desire, use Latisse only once a week to maintain and avoid eyelash loss.
Will I get more cleavage as I “drop and fluff”?
Cleavage
Apr 16, 2019
They will not drop closer together as you heal. The placement of the implants and your natural cleavage is based on your anatomy pre-op. They will, however, become softer as you heal and you will be able to push them together to form the look of the cleavage you desire.
3 weeks post-op. Do you think they are too big and too high? Would moderate profile have been better
Implant size
Apr 16, 2019
The implants will start to drop and settle into a more natural looking position starting at 3 weeks post op. The implants appearing “too high” is normal for this point in your recovery. As far as size, that is a personal preference. I will say 300-350cc round high profile is a very average size implant. Your PS might have you massage your implants down if necessary.

Textured surface implants are more resistant to motion and may have a bit more associated visible "traction" rippling but any implant will have some. this shows thru thin skin. the newer generation gel implants tend to ripple less but they are pretty firm. you should consider fat grafting to help soften the upper portion of your breasts. the options are numerous so best to get with your surgeon. best to you.
