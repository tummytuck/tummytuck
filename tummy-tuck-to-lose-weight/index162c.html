Tummy tuck by pounds and inches

The question does come up, especially after weight loss. How much weight can you expect to lose after tummy tuck, and how many inches will be lost from the waist.

Tummy tuck is all about abdominal shape and contour, adjusting the support and foundation through muscle plication and repair, and tailoring the closure so the skin excess is resolved and the scar is low and easily concealed.

The inches lost, or the weight of the skin removed is always a guess. Your final goal weight after tummy tuck, well that is your promise to keep. (Peter E. Johnson, MD, Peter E. Johnson, MD, Chicago Plastic Surgeon)

Weight loss and inches

Abdominoplasty to lose weight

Tummy tucks and liposuction are tools we use to shape and contour people’s midsections to look more attractive. Often weight loss of any significance is seldom achieved but that does not mean it is not worth doing. Rather than setting a goal of weight loss or inches, be aware that plastic surgeons are people too.

Although every attempt to improve your appearance is undertaken, it is not an exact science. When patients understand this, the results are often quite dramatic even though the scale would not predict much difference. (Timothy Fee, MD, Timothy Fee, MD, Jacksonville Plastic Surgeon)

With your weight and current measurements there is no way that an operation can reduce your waist by 10 inches, This would require a significant weight reduction and is probably something that will be very difficult for you to do. But you should first start with diet and exercise to see how much you can get off with that. It may give you a more achievable surgical goal. (John P. Stratis, MD, Harrisburg Plastic Surgeon)

How much can I reduce my waist with a tummy tuck?

The tummy tuck operation to lose weight

Without photos, it is pretty difficult to say exactly how many inches you can lose with a tummy tuck. In terms of actual weight loss, a tummy tuck doesn’t normally have a big impact on your weight unless you are having a significant amount of skin removed. If you are focusing more on fat loss, then you will lose very little weight since fat doesn’t weight much to begin with. My recommendation would be to consult with a local Board Certified Plastic Surgeon and review your expectations with them to see what they think you can actually achieve. (Gregory A. Buford, MD, FACS, Denver Plastic Surgeon)
