The scar location varies from person to person. The higher the belly button on the abdomen, the greater the likelihood of a scar above the bikini line, unless the “Vertical T” scar is used. This option relies on the TT being marked with the skin being pulled up “on stretch”. This creates the lowest possible scar tummy tuck. It is also a good idea to have your surgeon draw the incisions on your skin during your consultation, something we do on100% of our patients. When the drawn incision is pulled up, it shows the actual range of position of your final scar. Scar abdominoplasty

Patients are always worried about the scar left behind after tummy tuck. It is a very valid concern, since there is going to be a long scar tummy tuck. The trick is to place the scar such that it is hidden by under-garments or bathing suites. Patients should have a long conversation with their plastic surgeon about where scar tummy tuck may fall. Sometimes it is impossible to place the scar low due to lack of enough excess skin above the belly button. Also look at your surgeon’s before and after photos to see where he likes to place the scar. Every surgeon has his own way of performing the surgery, the photos can help you decide if the scar is worth the improvement in appearance from tummy tuck.

I utilize a technique for all my tummy tucks to hide the scar within the underwear line. I will often ask patients to wear an underwear or bikini that they desire the scar hidden within for their preoperative markings.
