Tummy tuck in San Antonio, Texas

Tummy tuck in San Antonio

Tummy tuck in San Antonio is a correction of the abdomen. Excess skin and fat is removed surgically from the abdominal area. Abdominoplasty is designed in case of violation of the right proportions of the anterior abdominal wall and trunk, which appeared due to muscle strain and thickening skinfold abdomen. Such changes occur, for example, in women with many children.

In addition, plastic surgeons of San Antonio do a tummy tuck with loss of elasticity of the skin, which appears due to excess weight, or age-related changes.

Plastic surgery has become a necessity for many women over 50 years old. Exercise, diet and medical cosmetics are not always able to correct the effects of aging. In such cases, cosmetic surgery of San Antonio comes to the aid to the woman. Let’s talk about the most popular types of correction.

The tummy tuck surgery in San Antonio

The tummy tuck surgery in San Antonio is performed under general anesthesia. The surgeon cuts the soft tissue around the navel and above the pubis to the bikini line, and then cuts the fat deposits and excess skin in the abdominal area.

The success of plastic surgery depends not only on the skill of the surgeon, but also on the individual patient, as well as the observance of all the doctor’s recommendations.

Before performing abdominoplasty, you need to drink a course of vitamins. The patient should be fully fed, be sure to include in your daily diet sufficient amount of meat, vegetables and fruit. It is desirable to lose excess weight. To do this, at the preparatory stage of the operation the patient is obliged to stick to the diet, go in for sport or do physical exercises. Smoking can disrupt the normal course of post-operative period, and also cause complications during surgery. Therefore, at least 2 weeks prior to abdominoplasty and for 2 weeks after it, you should not smoke. At least two weeks before the surgery, the patient should refuse to accept medicines that thin the blood, such as acetylsalicylic acid (aspirin).

After tummy tuck surgery in San Antonio

Contraindications to abdominoplasty are: heart and lung disorders; diabetes and other serious diseases of the internal organs; obese; a bleeding disorder; age less than 18 years, scheduled for next year pregnancy.

After surgery for 1-3 weeks can be observed abdominal pain, and within 2-3 weeks – general malaise, edema. Pain when moving will be passed within 2-3 months after surgery.

Within 1-3 weeks after surgery is reduced sensitivity in the seams, so you can not use a heating pad, take a hot shower or bath, sunbathing. In the first 10-14 days after surgery, you should regularly make dressings. Within a month after abdominoplasty is recommended to wear special clothes, bandage.
