Should I be worried about a rupture and silicone leaking?
should be okay
Feb 27, 2019
Breast implants can withstand a lot of trauma and pressure. The fact that you did not have bruising or swelling is a good sign. One cannot tell for sure if an implant is ruptured without an exam by an experienced plastic surgeon and some form of imaging such as a mammogram, ultrasound or MRI.
However, silicone implants can spontaneously rupture without any known trauma.
Do you have suggestions why my left breast is more swollen and harder?
wait it out
Feb 27, 2019
You may have a small hematoma around your implant on the left side. If it is getting better, I would wait and see if it resolves on its own. You may end up with everything okay. If you are worried and wish an assessment, I recommend that you see a trusted plastic surgeon for a personal examination and evaluation. Best of luck!
What is the Difference Between a MD and DO FACS?
MD versus DO

MD is a degree from a medical school, DO from an osteopathic school. Training is very similar at this time. I recommend you find a board certified plastic surgeon that does what you are looking for and you are comfortable with.
Breast Implant Removal Recovery
removal of implants under straight local
Nov 9, 2018
Yes, implants may be removed under straight local, fairly easily. Recovery will be quick and very little pain. Take 1-2 days off work, no heavy lifting for 2 weeks. I do not remove the capsule unless there is a medical reason to do it.
Need help choosing breast implant size! I’m a small chested 34A. Should I go with 350 or 400cc silicone i
What is best size 350 or 400?
Nov 9, 2018
It depends on your goals and wishes. Do you want a natural slope or more roundness up top?
It also depends on how your muscle and skin stretches to accommodate the implants. During surgery I would perform intraoperative sizing with a sizer and try out 350 and 400 cc and see what suits you the best and looks the best. That way you get the proper size that meets your goals.
I had my breast implants removed 7 1/2 months ago. Why do my breast still look concave and creased at the
concave and creased after breast implant removal
Nov 9, 2018
Your look is simply from stretched skin from the previous breast implant and lack of breast volume. When the implants were first removed, your body does a remarkable job of shrinking the loose skin of the breasts and reshaping the breasts. However, it may not do it completely and you have some residual loose skin. Your current look is probably not going to change much but I would probably wait at least 1 year and see how you feel about your appearance. If you are okay with it, then just live…
Can Liposuction and Tummy Tuck Be Done at the Same Time?
Liposuction and Tummy tuck
Nov 9, 2018
During a tummy tuck, liposuction is done of the abdomen and flanks(sides). This is normally part of the tummy tuck procedure and getting you the best result possible. In some situations if loose skin is the only problem, then liposuction may not be needed.
Frequently I will also perform liposuction of the mons pubis as part of my tummy tuck procedure.

breast explantation

Pain After Breast Lift and Augmentation – Is This Normal?
Searing pain after breast lift and augmentation
Nov 9, 2018
If your pain is searing or burning, this could be from your nerves that are stretched and not necessarily muscle pain. This will gradually improve over the next 1-2 weeks. Unfortunately, medications generally do not help this type of pain. It does improve over time.
How Long is Breast Implants Recovery Time?
Recovery after Breast Augmentation
Nov 9, 2018
Depends on what you need to get back to. Most patients will take 3-5 days off work and go back to work if they have a “desk job”. One week is a good enough time for recovery if going back to work you can avoid heavy lifting or just perform light duty. Generally any job that requires heavy lifting or repetitive exertion is going to take about 2 weeks recovery.

At What Age Do Your Breast Stop Developing?
Breasts Development
Nov 9, 2018
Normally breasts begin to develop at puberty, this can be as early as age 7-9 beginning with the breast bud. This can continue to age 16-18 and perhaps even to early 20’s. As you know the breasts continue to change as one gets older depending on factors such as weight gain or loss, exercise, medications and hormones, and of course pregnancy and breast feeding.
