Tummy tuck in Orange County is a surgical procedure that smoothes the stomach, removes excess fat, skin and strengthen muscles of the abdominal wall. Abdominoplasty differs from liposuction, although when necessary, tummy tuck in the center of plastic surgery can be performed in conjunction with liposuction.

Tummy tuck in Orange County, according to the type and volume, can take anywhere from one to five hours and is performed under general anesthesia. After tummy tuck procedure is necessary to limit physical activity for at least six weeks to allow for proper recovery. As a rule, most people love how they look after the procedure, but completely normal to feel after the operation is possible only in a few months.

Abdominoplasty removes excess fat, skin, and, in most cases, allows you to restore weakened or stretched muscles and create a smooth and elastic profile of the abdomen.

Another type of a tummy tuck in Orange County is the procedure cellulitis ectomy, which can be performed alone or in combination with abdominoplasty. Ectomy cellulitis involves the removal of the overhanging “apron” of skin and tissue below the navel. This excess skin is often the result of significant weight loss due to diet and exercise. Sagging skin of the abdomen is called pannus or “apron”, which can cause a variety of problems, including difficulties in complying with daily hygiene, infection or a cyst in the folds of the skin and low back pain. Unlike abdominoplasty, which restores the core muscles of the abdomen, with cellulitis ectomy removes only the excess skin and fat.

Ideal candidates for surgical tummy tuck in Orange County are those people who have excess abdominal skin and / or an abnormal amount of excess fat in the abdominal cavity. Almost all women after the birth of children have some degree of stretching of the abdominal muscles. Any of these conditions, or all three, can be corrected by the procedure of abdominoplasty in Orange County.

Abdominoplasty in Orange County

In the standard version, a horizontal incision of the abdomen is made just above the pubic hairline. Length of the lower abdominal incision determined by the amount of excess skin. The skin is dissected up to the chest, muscles are tightened, the navel is repositioned and excess skin is removed. In some cases, in order to achieve the best results, a plastic surgeon may also recommend to combine abdominoplasty with liposuction. There are three different types of surgical procedures, tummy tuck in Orange County: full abdominoplasty, a mini tummy tuck or partial abdominoplasty circular.

Tummy tuck orange county california 2

  • Full tummy tuck requires an incision above the pubic hairline, incision around the navel or belly button, and strengthening the muscles, arranged vertically on each side of the front wall of the abdominal cavity. With complete abdominoplasty can also be performed liposuction.
  • Partial or mini tummy tuck is recommended for patients with relatively little excess skin and muscle weakness of the abdominal wall. With partial abdominoplasty, you can avoid incision of navel . Liposuction procedure can also be performed during the operation.
  • Circular abdominoplasty in Orange County applies if there is a significant excess fat and skin in the back. Plastic surgery can be performed with liposuction of the back, including the area of the buttocks and thighs.

Despite the fact that a full recovery after a tummy tuck in Orange County will take several months, the result can be seen almost immediately, the new body contour will be permanent, and the weight is stable. An additional advantage for most patients is that the change in their weight did not lead to accumulation of fat in the waist as it was before the procedure. To minimize swelling and support the stomach after a tummy tuck procedure, the plastic surgeon may recommend wearing an elastic bandage or a compression garments.

It is important to follow the instructions of the surgeon. The patient will have to severely limit physical activity for four to six weeks to ensure a normal recovery and a good outcome. Most people tend to return to work within two weeks after the procedure in Orange County. All sutures are removed within a week.
