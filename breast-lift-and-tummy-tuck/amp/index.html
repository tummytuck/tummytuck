Breast surgery combined with tummy surgery

I very frequently perform breast lifts with and without implants with a tummy tuck. This procedure is usually known as a Mommy Makeover.

Breast lift and tummy tuck image

Combining the surgeries saves money and recovery time. If you do more than one procedure, the fees are usually discounted! Also, I would HIGHLY recommend staying in the country for surgery. If you have any problems afterward, it could get very expensive trying to find someone who will take care of you postoperatively. Therefore, you might want to consider getting both procedures done together right here in Baltimore or D.C. (Jeffrey E. Schreiber, MD, FACS, Baltimore Plastic Surgeon)

Tummy Tuck and Breast lift at Different Times

Unless you have an extremely complicated situation I would advise that you consider having both procedures together. The benefits of reducing the amount of anesthesia exposure and having only one recovery episode outweigh the downsides. Otherwise if you must have separate procedures I would recommend that you do the one that bothers you most first. (Mark Deutsch, MD, Atlanta Plastic Surgeon)

Order of surgeries

I would have to ask why you want to have one of the procedures overseas as I constantly caution patients against medical tourism. My personal preference is for tummy tuck first as I believe you will have a better idea of proportion and implant size once your abdomen is in shape. Depending on how much work is involved in each of the respective surgeries and surgical speed, it may be better to separate them with respect to anesthesia time and postop issues. (Robert L. Tummy tuck before or after breast surgery

Breast lift and tummy tuck surgery

This makes no sense to me. You are in a major city with access to excellent plastic surgeons and hospitals. If you are saving money by going abroad, please don’t. Going abroad gives you surgery without after-care which is essential for both your planned operations.If you do go abroad, set aside several thousand dollars more – to pay plastic surgeons in DC to care for you on your return. Such care is not free.

That said, if you are going to have two operations, have the tummy tuck and breast lift first. Then have the implants. Although implants are often done with lifts, there is a higher risk of infection when they are combined. So if two operations are done, lower your risk by separating the breast operations. (Elizabeth Morgan, MD, PhD, Atlanta Plastic Surgeon)
