Over or under muscle? Very fit and active. 5’3″, muscular, 140 lbs, with a B cup breast. W
Over or under muscle
Nov 4, 2018
Typically over muscle is associated with a bit quicker recovery and less movement of the breast implant with activity. This is balanced, in some cases, by a higher rate of capsular contracture. Above the muscle, these days, is typically subfascial. Both options have pros and cons and discussion with a board certified plastic surgeon to ensure you are comfortable with the implications of either approach may be your best move.
Can you decide after tissue expansion to go with tissue exchange instead of an implant?

Nov 4, 2018
Yes absolutely. In all cases it is critical for a board-certified plastic surgeon to determine your candidacy. But in most cases, tissue can be used to replace an implant. In our practice, the DIEP flap is most commonly used, but other options like the SIEA, latissimus, PAP, TUG, VUG, DUG, DCIA or TDAP flap may also be considered. You should see a plastic surgeon experienced in several of these options to help you make an informed decision.
