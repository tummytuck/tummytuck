Are my breasts tuberous or just saggy?
Jul 25, 2018
It looks as if you have what Plastic Surgeons call Pseudoptotic breasts. You are an excellent candidate for breast augmentation which will make your breasts appear more round and feminine. Stretch marks, however are permanent, and will not improve after surgery. Visit a board certified plastic surgeon for an examinattion and they will be able to make recommendations for your specific case.

Would a tummy tuck be the best decision?
Deciding if you need a tummy tuck
Jul 13, 2018
A full length torso photo is required before an opinion can be rendered regarding a tummy tuck, however tummy tucks are generally recommended for post-pregnancy patients like yourself with loose abdominal wall skin. Before you proceed with a tummy tuck, make certain you’ve decided to avoid future pregnancies. VIsit a board certified plastic surgeon and they will be able to give you a consultation about wether a tummy tuck is right for you.

What could a small lump under my areola be? I will be 5 months post op BA this month.
Small lump under areola after breast augmentation
Jul 11, 2018
Women have small glands under their areola called Montgomery glands that can get inflamed and possibly infected. I doubt this has any relationship with your surgery unless you’ve had your surgery performed via a periareolar approach. Speak to your Plastic Surgeon, who may advise antibiotics and seeing a breast surgeon.
What are these ripples: I am 3 weeks post-op and went 410cc under the muscle.
Post breast implant ripples
Jul 11, 2018
The photo indicates that you’ve undergone an augmentation/mastopexy, not a simple augmentation. Usually this procedure requires significant skin undermining, which is associated with skin swelling (edema) after surgery. Any compression (brassiere) on top of that will cause ripples. This should resolve by 6 weeks. If concerned, see your plastic surgeon.

Swelling after breast implants

The photo shows that you might have a postoperative seroma on the left, especially if you had a textured implant placed. I would recommend a simple ultrasound to rule that out. If the ultrasound shows a seroma, then please discuss this matter with your plastic surgeon.
Is this an infection or just really swollen? 10 days post-op tummy tuck, muscle repair and lipo
Post tummy tuck swelling and redness
Jul 11, 2018
It looks on the photo like you have a post operative seroma which should be aspirated. The redness may be a result of skin irritation from your compression binder. You should follow your surgeon's recommendations regarding antibiotics. I'd recommend contacting their office about how to proceed.
Is this capsular contracture causing radiating/numb arm pain? I'm 5 months post-op BA.
Arm pain after breast augmentation surgery
Jul 11, 2018
Damage to the intercostobrachial cutaneous nerve can cause radiating/numb upper inner arm pain. Capsular contracture, in continuity with the nerve, could also be exacerbating your symptoms. This is something you should discuss with your plastic surgeon.

Left breast only falls to side when laying down flat, 5 months post op 375cc silicone mod plus
Breast falls to the side when laying down
Jul 11, 2018
Your upright position is the position we judge the placement of your breast implants and in that position all seems correct. A normal unaugmented breast falls to the side when you lie flat, so its normal for your augmented breast to fall sideways when you are lying down as well. Sometimes wearing brassiere 24/7 encourages a more symmetrical result by preventing side displacement. If you're concerned about this after six months, consult your plastic surgeon.
400/375cc ultra high profile. or 340/310cc?
Choosing between implant sizes
Jun 26, 2018
Your question cannot be answered without a thorough review of your photos and your breast/chest wall measurements. Generally, choosing between a 375 and a 340 will have minimal differences, but choosing between 400 and 310 will make a difference. See a Board Certified Plastic Surgeon and they will be able to guide you.

Making a realistic breast implant size decision

Yes, Charlotte, this is a realistic “wish boob” for you judging by your photos. Your existing breasts are asymmetric in size and shape, so you may require different size and shaped implants for optimal results. You have a sizable breast to start, so a good Plastic Surgeon should be able to meet your B.F. goals.
