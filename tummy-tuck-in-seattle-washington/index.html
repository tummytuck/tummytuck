What is a tummy tuck in Seattle?

Seattle, WA tummy tuck scar

Abdominoplasty is a type of cosmetic surgery in Seattle, aimed at restoring or improving the shape of the abdomen. The main object of cosmetic surgery is to remove excess skin and fatty tissue deposits to form the desired shape and proportions. The procedure also allows you to get rid of post-surgical scars and sagging of abdominal fascia. Such interference can not be regarded as an alternative to bariatric surgery (surgical treatment of obesity) . However abdominoplasty in Seattle can effectively eliminate local cosmetic defect, giving the body a perfect shape and is used as a standalone procedure or in conjunction with other plastic surgery.

Medical reasons for tummy tuck in Seattle

Abdominoplasty in Seattle recommended in the following cases:

Seattle WA tummy tuck before and after

  • Stretching of the abdominal fascia after pregnancy
  • Weakness of the lower portion of the abdominal wall
  • Scar after cesarean section or other surgery on the stomach
  • Congestion and sagging of abdominal skin after significant weight loss, even after bariatric surgery
  • Improving the shape of the abdomen after liposuction
  • Age-related changes associated with a decrease in skin elasticity

Tummy tuck surgery in Seattle

Abdominoplasty Seattle WA

Seattle abdominoplasty performed under general anesthesia as part of a day hospital or short-term stay (depending on the volume of procedure and the general state of the patient). The surgical procedure takes about three hours. During surgery, the plastic surgeon performs several cuts skin, subcutaneous tissue and abdominal fascia. Fascia is sutured with strong seams that prevent its sagging, which gives a flat stomach and an elastic appearance, then remove excess fat and skin. After the procedure, overlap cosmetic seams in inconspicuous places, ensuring minimal scarring in the natural folds, invisible to the naked eye.

Tummy tuck post op in Seattle

Seattle WA tummy tuck surgery scar

In the postoperative period may be mild abdominal pain that can be treated effectively with modern anesthetics. During surgery, drainage tubes are inserted in the sections providing outflow discharge from the wounds to accelerate the healing process and prevent infection. Drains are removed with termination of blood discharge.

Medical staff of Seattle clinic helps get out of bed as soon as possible and restore full mobility, that it is necessary to prevent thrombus formation in the lower extremities. In some cases, in the postoperative period, experts may recommend injecting use of anticoagulants (funds that prevent blood clotting) to prevent thrombosis.

At this time is necessary to avoid significant physical exertion, that is accompanied by tilt and tension of the skin. The final results of abdominoplasty can be assessed three months after the procedure, with a decrease in postoperative edema and restoration of normal proportions of the abdomen.
