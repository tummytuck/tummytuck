If you do decide to go with a tummy tuck first it is advisable that you wait a minimum of 4-6 months so that your body has time to properly heal before undergoing the stress of carrying a child.

Tummy tuck is an out-patient surgery perform to remove the extra skin and tissue from the abdomen to flatten the area as much as possible. It also usually involves tightening of the abdominal muscles. The tummy tuck procedure is best performed when patient is at a stable weight and usually after pregnancies are finished. There is no reason why patient can not have another pregnancy after tummy tuck, but this will cause more laxity of the skin and will have a negative effect on the result. Also if muscle tightening has been done, fullness of the pregnancy may be higher on the abdomen. But not to worry it will have no effect on pregnancy itself.

Is it possible to do tummy tuck before pregnancy?

Yes, it is possible to get tummy tuck before pregnancy. Although the tummy tuck has little affect to a potential pregnancy or baby, the skin/muscle will stretch and loosen with the pregnancy.

For this reason, it is recommended that most patients wait to have a tummy tuck until they are done with their childbearing years. If they change their mind, we recommend waiting 1 year from the tummy tuck procedure to allow for a comfortable pregnancy.

It is recommended that you complete your family prior to having a tummy tuck (Standard abdominoplasty). A tummy tuck will remove extra skin and tighten remaining skin on the abdomen. Muscle repair is performed to assist in the goal of achieving a flat tummy. If there is a change of life plan or a surprise pregnancy you need not worry about you or the baby. A secondary tummy tuck is possible but can be more difficult due to existing scar tissue.

A full tummy tuck should optimally tighten up the skin of your abdomen. Most of the time, muscle tightening is also accomplished. Pregnancy occurring after a tummy tuck should be expected to reverse some of the positive effects of your abdominoplasty surgery.

Yes you can have another baby after a tummy tuck. However, you may diminish the results of the surgery by the weight gain during pregnancy. The abdominal muscles which are pulled back together in a tummy tuck, may stretch apart if you had another baby.

Weight gain may cause additional stretch marks and loose skin on the abdomen. I recommend that women be done having kids before investing the money in having a tummy tuck.

It is a good idea to differ having tummy tucks until at least nine months after the last child birth. However under unpredictable/uncontrolable condition it is quite safe to undergo tummy tucks 9-12 months after any given child birth, tummy tucks would not interfere with pregnancies.
