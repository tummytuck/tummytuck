Can a plastic surgeon require a certain BMI to perform a tummy tuck?
A BMI of less than 30 is recommended to minimize the risks of surgery
Oct 24, 2017
It is well-recognized that the higher your BMI is, they risk of complications increases. In general, a BMI of less than 30 is recommended to minimize the risks of surgery including delayed wound healing, wound infection, DVT, and pulmonary embolism as well as to optimize the outcome.
Can liposuction and tummy tuck be done after a c-section?
​Tummy tucks are commonly performed in patient who have previously had a C-section
Oct 24, 2017
Tummy tucks are commonly performed in patient who have previously had a C-section. The tummy tuck surgery often removes the existing C-section scar. It is advisable to delay the tummy tuck surgery until you no longer plan to have more children because it is unpredictable how your skin and muscle will react with pregnancy following a tummy tuck and you may end up needing another tummy tuck.
Swelling related. Can this get worse?
Possible Fluid Collection (Seroma)
Oct 24, 2017
Swelling following a tummy tuck may persist up to 3 months but most should resolve within a few weeks. Wearing an abdominal binder helps to decrease the swelling. If you feel that your swelling is progressively getting worse, you should follow up with your plastic surgeon to make sure that you don't have a fluid collection (seroma) building up.
During the Tummy tuck procedure, how does the doctor get an accurate result if I'm lying d
Markings Are Done Preoperatively
Mar 15, 2016
You are right in that the body appears very differently lying down vs standing up.  In an abdominoplasty, markings for the surgery are done preoperatively with the patient standing up and adjustments are then made during surgery with the patient lying down.  Often, the patient is flexed during surgery to maximize the amount of skin removal.  A surgeon who is experienced at performing abdominplasties will be able to make this determination during surgery.
Swelling with Tummy tuck. Any suggestions?
Compression Garment and Silicone Gel Sheeting
Mar 10, 2016
Swelling following an abdominoplasty, especially if you also have liposuction, may last up to 3-6 months.  Wearing a compression garment can often help with the swelling.  In terms of scars, massaging, silicone gel sheeting, and pressure have been shown to be effective for the appearance of scars.
Can you have an arm lift and tummy tuck at the same time?
Multiple surgeries can be safely combined if they are done in a timely manner
Feb 24, 2016
Multiple surgeries can be safely combined if they are done in a timely manner. The risk of complications increase the longer the surgeries are. If the surgeries can be performed under six hours, they may be safely combined. You should visit a board-certified plastic surgeon with experience doing multiple body contouring procedures.

When is the best time to return to the gym after arm lift surgery?
Six Weeks
Feb 22, 2016
Typically, you should wait 6 weeks before doing any heavy lifting or participate in rigorous physiccal activities.  It is important that you slowly transition yourself and increase the activity level as tolerated.  With brachioplasty, patients sometimes have scar contracture, especially in the axillar, that may limit their range of motion.  You should talk to you plastic surgeon before starting any heavy physical activity.

Common to Have Post-Operative Swelling
Feb 19, 2016
It is common to have post-operative swelling following an abdominoplasty and liposuction.  The swelling may take months to completely resolve.  Wearing compression garments can help with swelling.  I often tell patients to wait at least 3-6 months before determining the final results of an abdominoplasty and liposuction.  If your swelling  progressively worsen or if you have significant pain, redness, drainage, or fever, you should inform your plastic surgeon.
