Tummy tuck in Fayetteville NC assumes elimination of excess of hypodermic and fatty cellulose, and also a part of the stretched muscles and surplus of skin in a stomach, for the purpose of giving to it esthetically attractive look.

Medical reasons for tummy tuck in Fayetteville NC

  • the skin and fatty apron formed by stretched muscles of the stomach and a superfluous fatty fabric;
  • considerable weight loss with sagging of fabrics of a stomach;
  • a large number of extensions of the skin, remained after pregnancy;
  • available hems in the lower part of a stomach, for example, after Cesarean section or other operations;
  • divergence of direct muscles of a stomach after the delivery;
  • umbilical hernia.

Existence of umbilical hernia is, as a rule, complicating plasticity of a stomach a factor, but it does not mean that intervention is impossible.

Contra-indications to tummy tuck in Fayetteville NC

All contra-indications are not absolute and possibility of carrying out surgery is considered in each case especially.

Preparing for tummy tuck in Fayetteville NC

Consultations are an important link in the chain of preparatory activities. During the consultation the doctor carries out an objective examination, collects detailed information about the habits and lifestyle, explains the features and risks of intervention, as well as answers to all the questions that you care. Including:

  • tells of used anesthesia , its features;
  • explains the essence of the surgery, and if there is no objection from your side, describes the stages of its implementation;
  • finds out presense an uncontrolled conditions such as surges in blood pressure, heart rhythm failures, the propensity to form keloids, etc. ;

In addition there are a number of recommendations that need to be done in the preparatory phase:

Tummy tuck scar in Fayetteville, NC

  • reduce weight, if it is excessive and may impede for quality performing surgery and achieving their desired an aesthetic effect;
  • if after the last birth has not yet been 12 months, it is better to wait with the surgery and allow tissues of the abdomen to minimize independently;
  • if stomach tissue too stretched, then 2-3 weeks prior to surgery may be assigned wearing a bandage, which will perform the adaptation function;
  • 1-2 weeks prior to surgery to stop taking aspirin or other means which impair coagulability of blood;
  • quit smoking 2 weeks before the intervention to reduce the risk of serious complications such as necrosis of the wound edges: smoking violates the microcirculation in the injured tissues, which significantly impairs their healing;
  • during the menstruation and a few days before them surgery is not done, so try as much as possible take this into account;
  • in some cases for two days prior to surgery, is prescribed starvation and enemas for a better bowel movement, which is essential for such intervention;
