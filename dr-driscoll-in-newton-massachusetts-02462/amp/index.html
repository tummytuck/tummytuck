If isolated neck lift “dog ears,” how is it corrected?
dog ear correction
Nov 11, 2017
When ever skin is removed the amount in the center is "cheated out" along the incision in something of an ellipse. The shorter the ellipse the shorter the incision and the more likely the 'dog ear' redundancy at the ends. to correct it just means the incision has to be made longer. the likelihood of a dogear is also dependent on the elasticity of the skin, as they tend to get much better over time in people with good skin elasticity. good luck, hope this helps.

Is the constant presence of this hard bulge normal 4 weeks post tummy tuck?
persistent bulge
Nov 10, 2017
Thanks for the question. the treatment depends on the diagnosis. A persistent bulge can be fluid between the skin and abdominal wall, such as a seroma, or can just be edema or fluid within the tissues. Seromas will need a drainage and compression, edema just needs time and compression. other reasons for a bulge exist as well, such as laxity of the abdominal. Get your diagnosis and then get the appropriate treatment. Good luck.
27 years old done having babies do you think I’m a good candidate for a tummy tuck?
higher BMI tummy tucks
Nov 10, 2017
Thanks for your question. I think you would have safer surgery if yo were to lose some weight. This will allow for a decrease in complications of delayed healing, seroma, infection, and of course more skin can be removed if done at a better weight. That being said, you are a candidate if weight loss is basically not likely event. Surgeons will often mitigate complication rates by less undermining of the skin or tightening of the abdomen. Good luck!!
