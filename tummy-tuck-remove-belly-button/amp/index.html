Tummy tuck remove belly button

The belly button usually has a scar around it after a full tummy tuck. It does not actually move in most cases – the skin is pulled over it and a new hole is made for it to show, thus the scar.

This allows skin to be tightened above the belly button. This contrasts a mini tummy tuck, which does not leave a scar around the belly button.

A mini tummy tuck only tightens skin below the belly button. (Michael Vennemeyer, MD, Southlake Plastic Surgeon)

In a classic full TT, the navel is not moved. Rather it is left in place, an incision is made around it, the procedure is done, the abdominal skin pulled down, and an opening made over the navel to which it is sutured.

In a mini-TT the navel is left in place, and only the excess skin below the navel is removed. It is possible to tighten the abdominal muscles all the way up to the chest.

In an “umbilical float” procedure, the navel is lifted off the abdomen with the rest of the abdominal skin and fat, and sutured down often at a slightly lower position.

Belly Buttons Usually NOT removed during Tummy Tuck

The short answer is that Belly Buttons Usually NOT removed during Tummy Tuck procedures. Rather it remains in place with its blood supply intact. The skin and soft tissues are freed up around the belly button, brought down like a shade and the redundancy removed.

The muscles are usually tightened as needed if separated in the middle and a new opening is made for the original belly button to pop through. Making a smaller hole will result in a smaller belly button which pops out in the same position as it was more or less to begin with. (Larry S. Nichter, MD, MS, FACS, Orange County Plastic Surgeon)
