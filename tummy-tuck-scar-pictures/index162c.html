Tummy tuck is aimed at improving the aesthetic appearance of a particular area of ​​the body. But even after such surgeries scars remain. At observance of all rules of the recovery period will inevitably appear scars after plastic surgery. This is caused by soft tissue healing in place suture the incision. In most cases, these scars do not cause any problems.

On the tummy tuck scar pictures you see the scar that are hiding between the folds of the skin, becoming virtually invisible. Specific trouble delivering scars after plastic surgery, the formation of which is naturally for some reason disturbed. As a consequence, there are hypertrophic or keloid scars, which you can also see on the tummy tuck scar pictures. When excessive proliferation of connective tissue formed hypertrophic scars, towering above the skin. Depending on the type of scar apply different correction methods.

Hypertrophic scars should begin to treat as early as possible, keloid contrary, only after complete healing and scar formation process. But in both cases for the treatment is applied electrophoresis and phonophoresis. It is actively destroying the basic components rumen – collagen fibers and hyaluronic acid both early and late stages of scarring. Effective treatment of scars after surgery depends on the correct choice of the method of exposure. Laser treatment – the best remedy for scars after surgery.

Possibly you know that the basis of all the scarring after surgery, wounds, burns, acne removal is collagen. And in order to remove scars or even reduce the possibility of their occurrence, it is necessary to use methods that with using of enzymes break down the excess collagen. On the market a lot of these enzyme preparations. But most of them are very toxic. Or not fully provide the desired aesthetic result.

Scarring is a problem, when the scars are formed such as:

  • lumpy scars,
  • depressed scars,
  • keloid scars (with disorderly accumulation of collagen),
  • itchy welts,
  • painful scars,
  • scars with color distortion (very light or dark).

What affects the formation of a tummy tuck scar?

  • Location of wounds in the functionally active zone. Incorrect wound healing.
  • The location of the wound. The depth of the wound.
  • Racial and individual characteristics. Problems of improper scarring are more common in redheads and dark-skinned people.
  • The diseases associated with metabolic disorders such as diabetes, atherosclerosis, vitamin deficiencies.
  • Genetic predisposition.
  • Age features in adults scars less noticeable than in children and adolescents.
    impact factors – ultraviolet, X-ray irradiation, wound infection.
