Liposuction is a reasonable option, depending on your goals. You appear to have limited extra skin. A physical exam may change that observation. The scar burden with tummy tuck treatment should always be considered, which your consultant has obviously done.

Would you have extra skin after liposuction? Probably, but the amount of extra skin does not justify the scar burden for some patients. A tummy tuck treatment is also a reasonable recommendation if you were not concerned about the scar burden.

A Tummy tuck treatment

If you were unhappy with the excess skin after liposuction, then you could always get a tummy tuck at a later time. As a general rule, after having children, skin on the abdomen does not tighten or shrink significantly after liposuction. The stretch marks could indicate poor skin tone/elasticity, in which case there would be very little shrinkage after liposuction.

It is important to understand that surgeons recommend a specific procedure(s) based on the goals the patient articulates during the consultation. Depending upon the goals you expressed to the surgeon, either procedure is reasonable, and neither procedure is ideal. In other words, the surgeon’s recommendation may change based on on the bias conveyed by the patient.
