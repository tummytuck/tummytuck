RealSelf Q&A with Dr. Edelstein

RS: What inspired you to become a surgeon?
JE: I found that surgery was more involved than other medical specialties—the technical aspect of it and how you get to use your hands. I considered other surgical specialties, but I was blown away by the variety of plastic surgery—not just cosmetic, but reconstructive surgery as well, which I often still do.

RS: What’s a consultation with you like?
JE: A consultation at my Toronto office is about getting to know patients and learning their goals and desires. Patients are never rushed during a consultation—I want them to be comfortable and well-informed, and I always take the time to answer questions and understand their expectations. The bottom line is that I love what I do, I care about my patients, and I want them to know that I’ll do my very best for them.

RS: What sets your practice apart?
JE: In addition to focusing on just a few procedures to reach an elite level of expertise, we try to be sure every patient has what we call a Four Seasons experience. Like the hotel’s dedication to the highest standards, we want to provide the ultimate patient experience from beginning to end, in terms of service and quality of the surgery.

RS: How would you describe your approach to plastic surgery?
JE: Transformative aesthetic surgery is definitely a merger of art and science, but, overall, I’d describe my process as being meticulous. With breast augmentation, for example, I take a lot of measurements in order to figure out what breast implants fit the person properly.

I really pay attention to details and don’t leave anything to chance. For instance, while little puckers of skin where the incision ends (“dog ears”) are common after tummy tucks and often go away after a while, I correct them there and then. Some surgeons are more laid-back, but I sweat the small stuff.

Would I be a good candidate for a mini tummy tuck and lipo as well?
Mini-Abdominoplasty Candidate?
Jan 31, 2020
Hello, 
Thank you for your question and photos. Mini tummy tucks typically don’t address the laxity in the upper abdomen. Based on your photos, you may want to consider a full tummy tuck. I’d recommend booking a consultation with a Board Certified Plastic Surgeon to discuss the best method to achieve your desired result.

All the best.

Do you use blood thinners after a tummy tuck with all patients?
Blood Thinners After Abdominoplasty
Jan 31, 2020
Hello,Thank you for your question.  I don’t typically prescribe blood thinners for patients after a tummy tuck.  It elevates the risk for bleeding.  I normally encourage patients to be mobile and not spend all their time laying in bed after surgery.  Being ambulatory will help minimize the risks of developing deep clots in the leg veins.  It’s best to speak with your board certified plastic surgeon, as every surgeon has different post-operative protocols.All the best.
