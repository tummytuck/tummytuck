Tummy Tuck or Bariatric Surgery? I’m not sure what is best for me. Need an honest opinion from ex
weight loss first
Sep 8, 2020
It is always best to be at or near your lowest weight before having a tummy tuck. It will certainly reduce your risk of complications, and it will result in a much better result from your surgery. I don’t usually give patients a certain weight that they must get to, but it is important for you to reduce weight in order to make a tummy tuck a safe operation for you. Do I need revision surgery? If so, why?
botttoming out
Jan 22, 2019
Implants always have the possibility of displacement after surgery.  The risk is reduced with careful dissection of the pocket, and use of smaller implants.  The use of support bras after surgery can also help reduce this risk.  You have bottoming out of the left side.  This can usually be repaired with some internal stitches (capsulorrhaphy).  Occasionally additional support needs to be added with an additional product sutured in to add support.

Is it best to get a general surgeon for abdominal separation or a plastic surgeon?
diastasis recti
Nov 16, 2018
General surgeons are very skilled at fixing hernias.  They rarely treat distasis recti.  Muscle separation without a hernia is usually not covered by insurance.  This falls well within the common practice of most plastic surgeons.  We commonly repair diastasis during nearly every tummy tuck that we perform.  I would recommend that you see a board certified plastic surgeon to be examined and get the best advice for repair.
