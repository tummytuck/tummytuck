Abdominoplasty after baby is usually performed under general anesthesia (ie, during the surgery you will sleep). Also, surgery can be performed under local anesthesia with the application of a sedative. The length of the cut depends on the number of removed skin. Then there is a removal and adjustment of adipose tissue after which the skin is separated from the abdominal wall and lifted, exposing the rectus abdominis.
