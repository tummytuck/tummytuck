Dangling belly is quite an unpleasant sight, both for its owner, and for others. From an excess accumulation of fat in the abdominal area anybody is not insured, that is why tummy tuck is equally in demand among both men and women. Should i get a tummy tuck surgery

Tummy tuck is a surgical procedure for a stomach. Thus, the tummy tuck is an aesthetic surgery and serves primarily for figure correction. It is aimed at the removal of excess skin and fat in the abdominal area to restore normal aesthetic proportions.

Typically, this surgery used by women after childbirth or dramatic weight loss, since in these cases the abdominal wall tissues often remain stretched and weakened. Also, tummy tuck can be performed on overstretched skin of the abdomen, when detected striae (stretch marks), or with extensive postoperative scarring. Abdominoplasty is done by both women and men.

When should i get a tummy tuck

Of course, the implementation of tummy tuck is not shown for all patients who have excess fat in the abdominal area. If the amount of body fat is not too large, often enough just to attend classes at the gym or dieting. Physical activity in combination with diet may be the way out for the most part of people, but alas, not for everyone. Sports can consume a lot of time and effort, in its turn diets are often associated with different side effects.

Explicit indications for a tummy tuck are:

  • skin and fat apron and expressed folds;
  • ptosis (drooping) of the anterior abdominal wall;
  • excess skin and subcutaneous tissue of the anterior abdominal wall, which can not be removed by other means and methods (diet, exercise, liposuction, a combination of these techniques);
  • presence of pronounced stretch marks (striae pale) with an excess of abdominal skin;
  • discrepancy recti and general stretching of the abdominal muscles;
  • rough postoperative scars in the lower abdomen;
  • significant subcutaneous fat in the abdomen;
  • weakening of the muscles of the anterior abdominal wall;
  • excess sagging skin on the abdomen, the presence of “apron”;
  • the anterior abdominal wall hernia, diastasis recti, scar deformity;

When i should not get a tummy tuck

Should you get a tummy tuck

The general contraindications to tummy tuck include:

  • heart and lung disorders;
  • diabetes and other serious diseases of internal organs;
  • marked obesity;
  • a bleeding disorder;
  • age less than 18 years;
  • planned within the next year pregnancy;
  • acute infectious and viral disease;
  • organic diseases of the cardiovascular and respiratory systems in the stage of decompensation;
  • organic diseases of the nervous system;
  • intestinal disorders;
  • uncompensated diabetes mellitus;
  • allergic diseases;
  • pregnancy.

Local tummy tuck contraindications include:

  • fungal infections of the skin;
  • dermatitis, purulent skin diseases;
  • psoriasis.

Tummy tuck is contraindicated for women who are soon planning a pregnancy. Also, surgery can not be performed when taking certain medications and drugs.
