When a tummy tuck done in Maryland?

Indications for surgery of tummy tuck are:

  • Tension (laxity) of the skin of the anterior abdominal wall with the presence of stretch marks in the lower abdomen;
  • Diastasis (divergence) of recti combined with stretching the skin of the anterior abdominal wall;
  • Availability skinfold in the lower abdomen;
  • Scarring of the skin of the anterior abdominal wall (multiple extensions), the so-called scar atrophy of the skin of the abdomen.

Postoperative period after abdominoplasty

Before and after tummy tuck in Maryland

Md tummy tuck scar big picture

During this period it is important to observe two basic rules. Firstly necessary to achieve an adequate immobilization of tissues in the region surgery, on the other hand it requires early activity of the patient. Reliable tissue immobilization is achieved by wearing special compression stockings, and a few bent posture of the patient, as in an upright position and in the supine position. During the first two days, the patient is in the hospital in Maryland under the supervision of a doctor and get all the necessary treatment. Bed rest is required for 2 days. The patient lies in bed with a raised head and foot end.

Discharge from the hospital is carried out on the third day after surgery.

Within 1-2 weeks the patient stays at home, regularly visiting the doctor to perform dressings and for inspections. In the first days after surgery the patient should walk hunched over, to remove excessive tension on the edges of the surgical wound. Wearing of compression stockings is required for 1-2 months depending on the volume of performed surgery .

Sutures are removed not earlier than 14 days after the tummy tuck in Maryland.

The final outcome of abdominoplasty surgery can be judged not earlier than six months after the surgery.

Possible complications after a tummy tuck surgery in Maryland.

Abdominoplasty Maryland

As with any surgical procedure, complications can be both general and local.

General complications:

  • Overload of the pulmonary circulation during overly broad suturing fascia of the anterior abdominal wall.
  • Postoperative physical inactivity of patient. Such complications as deep vein thrombosis of the lower limbs, hypostatic pneumonia, are the result of immobilization of the patient in the early postoperative period. Early activation of the patient is necessary for the prevention of these complications.

Local complications:

Maryland Tummy tuck

  • Hematoma (accumulation of blood in the area of surgery).
  • Seroma (accumulation of fluid in the wound).
  • Necrosis (mortification) of the wound edges. Causes of necrosis of the wound edges are: too extensive detachment of skin-fat flaps of the anterior abdominal wall, the imposition of seams on the skin with a large tension, the presence of postoperative scarring of the skin of the abdomen. The main measure to prevent necrosis of the skin edges during abdominoplasty – rational detachment of skin-fat flaps with the characteristics of each individual patient.
  • Suppuration after the surgical wound.
