Can I lose an abdominal apron without surgery?

When a patient has a hanging apron of fat in the lower abdomen, further weight loss and exercising, though perhaps desirable, will not get rid of it.

You are an ideal candidate for a standard abdominoplasty, which tightens the muscles and exchanges the fold for a transverse scar in the bikini line.

This surgery is performed under a general anesthetic in an ambulatory setting. You may have drains for a few days and you should be able to return to work in 2 weeks. Exercising may be resumed in 6 weeks. (J. Brian Boyd, MD, Los Angeles Plastic Surgeon)

After weight loss, is there any non-surgical way to make your tummy flat?

The honest answer is that there are no non-surgical procedures that are going to make your stomach completely flat. Some skin tightening methods may improve it slightly but nothing other than a tummy tuck will give you a drastic result. I think you would be an ideal candidate for a full abdominoplasty, but I would wait until you are within 5-10 pounds of your goal weight. Consult a board-certified plastic surgeon when you are ready to take that step. (Brian Reedy, MD, Reading Plastic Surgeon)

Flat stomach without surgery?

You have a fair amount of skin laxity which is quite common with weight loss or post pregnancy. Unfortunately, there is no non-surgical method of tightening skin to this extent. There are some radio-frequency methods of a small amount of skin tightening with many repeated treatments such as Endymed, but you would not get very much tightening with your degree of laxity, and would just be wasting your money. Your best outcome will be a full tummy tuck from a skilled surgeon, although you will get improvement with a mini tummy tuck as well. (Pablo Prichard, MD, Phoenix Plastic Surgeon)
